The original dataset made by Adam Halper, has collected 150,000,000 survey responses which when normalised, allows us to find correlations whether social media affects shopping or not as well as who does it directly affect. To normalise the data of 1451 entries. I used a simple sumif formulae with 2 parameters, one for females and one to pick the type of social media for each gender given. (=SUMIFS(E2:E1451, D2:D1451, "Facebook", C2:C1451, "Male voters")). This was then done for every social media platform for both genders. As we can see, Facebook, Snapchat, and Twitter have a bigger effect on males' shopping habits than females. However, Instagram effects females' shopping habits more than males.
